Project Overview

To come up with a unique project is something I believe to be very hard in this day and age, though there are many defining features and functions that can make it stand out.

After carefully considering and weighing my options, I've decided to go with a smartphone app created specifically for Motorbike riders. This app will provide riders with great scenic roads and facilities along the way ie. Petrol Stations, Restaurants, and Look-outs to visit based on an entered location or the GPS location of the device. Other features will include the ability to create a profile, form a group and connect with other riders, track routes and send alerts for any safety hazards/cautions on the way. Along with this, the app will provide information through voice notifications (text-to-speech).

For many years now the commonality of motorcycles in Australia has been on a steady rise. Statistics show that from 2012 to 2021, the number of registered motorcycles nationwide increased from 709,288 to 913,803. By the end of 2022, evidence suggests that the number will break one million. Considering the fact that there are 20.1 million cars currently registered, this is a very minute amount. Moving forward, I strongly believe that there should be more applications tailored to motorcycle riders of all ages and experience levels.

Description

Building on from above, this app will not only serve as a tool to increase the quality of life for motorcyclists of all experiences but also provide a safe social space for those who may be learners looking for advice and practice. Using GPS satellite technology and an interactive map, users will be able to locate and receive a route to their chosen destination; a list of suggested places nearby will also be included.

As well as being able to find locations and get directions, motorcyclists will be able to track their own ride as well as their group members. In recent times, motorcycle helmets with blue-tooth headset compatibility have slowly been becoming the norm. In support of this, the app will feature voice command options and text-to-speech so you don’t need to keep glancing down at your phone. Safety is another very important feature, so an alert system will be implemented; this can either be done in the app or from a voice command. This will show up as a “caution” sign on your map.

To take this concept one step further, the addition of a “track” mode will be built in. This side of the app will allow the user to input a track they are currently riding at and record their lap times. You will have the option to either publish your time or keep it private (other app users' times will be recorded and displayed).

Tools and Technology

To create this app, there are quite a few tools and technologies needed.

They will be (and not limited to):

  • GPS (Global Positions System); Receives information from currently visible satellites to display current location. The system accurately calculates and coordinates where you are situated no matter where that is.
  • Cell-ID; a generally unique number used to identify each base transceiver station.
  • A-GPS (Assisted GPS); The integration of GPS and Cell-ID. Combined, these provide highly accurate data.
  • Geofencing; Refers to a GPS system and determines the closeness of a user to a particular location.
  • Framework such as Ionic; visually pleasing, easy to use and widely supported.
  • Node.js; is used for the back-end, and focuses on mainly real-time events. Ie. chats, instant messages, likes, and comments. Could consider using PHP-based frameworks like Symphony2.
  • Databases such as JSON, SQLite, NoSQL etc.
  • Third-party admin panel.
  • Cloud Server Service; for example, AWS. Used for processing workloads and storing information. Can access virtual server functions remotely through an online interface.
  • A programming language such as Swift; Used for iOS devices & successor to Objective-C (could be used for compatibility with Android as well).

    I believe if this project is successful, motorcycle riders will enjoy the ability to be able to do all their needed searching, researching and socialising all in one application; without needing to search Google or other third-party websites.
